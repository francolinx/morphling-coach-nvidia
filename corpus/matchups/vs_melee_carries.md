# Morphling vs Melee Physical Carries

## Why melee carries threaten Morphling in lane

Melee physical carry matchups — primarily Ursa, Slark, and Phantom Assassin — present a distinct threat from ranged or mage-based opponents. These heroes walk onto Morphling and force right-click trades where raw burst and sustain beat Morphling's attribute-swap toolkit before it comes online. **Ursa** is the hardest in-lane matchup: Fury Swipes stacks multiplicative bonus physical damage on consecutive hits, meaning by the third right-click at level 3-4, Ursa is dealing 150+ bonus damage per hit. Morphling cannot out-sustain Fury Swipes through Attribute Shift alone because the burst happens faster than the shift rate, and Fury Swipes stacking ignores armor — high agility does not protect you the way it would against a normal right-clicker. **Slark** punishes Waveform directly: Pounce's leash is a root that blocks movement abilities, so a well-timed Pounce prevents Waveform from firing or traps Morphling mid-flight. Post-7.38 Slark with the Dark Reef Renegade facet applies armor reduction on attacks, accelerating physical damage against a hero who shifts into low strength. Diffusal Blade (Slark's core item) burns mana, stripping both Attribute Shift and Waveform from Morphling's toolkit simultaneously. **Phantom Assassin** is lower-threat at levels 1-5 but spikes sharply at level 6 with Coup de Grâce crits; her Stifling Dagger also deals 40% of attack damage as physical damage at range, letting her whittle Morphling without stepping into Waveform range.

## Survival strategy: 0-10 minutes

The universal rule in all three matchups is **do not trade — farm through them**. Keep Waveform off cooldown against Ursa and Slark: in this matchup Waveform is not a farming tool, it is your exit; every Waveform cast on a creep wave opens a 14-10 second kill window for a diving Ursa or Pouncing Slark. Pre-shift defensively — when a melee carry walks up to hit you, start Agi→Str immediately before the first hit lands; waiting until you are being hit means you absorb the burst at your existing stat allocation since the shift rate at level 3-4 is slow. Rush Morbid Mask as first item into any hard melee lane: lifesteal off creeps lets you recover HP between aggression windows without burning mana on Attribute Shift, buying time to hit Yasha or Manta timing. Respect Ursa's level-6 Enrage window: with Enrage active (80% damage reduction) and stacked Fury Swipes, Ursa can kill a full-HP Morphling in under 3 seconds — when Ursa hits 6, back off the creep wave until Enrage expires or a support can threaten. Against Slark, use fog before Waveform: step into lane-edge trees to break vision first, then Waveform to safety; if Slark Pounces first Waveform is locked, so using the fog step avoids the leash window entirely.

## When to TP vs when to fight

Against Ursa or Slark at level 6 when you are not: TP out freely — your win condition is gold and levels, not lane kills into these matchups. Request a support rotation if they are diving aggressively; counter-pressure only after Morbid Mask plus your first major item (Yasha or Manta core). Against PA, contest the lane more actively at levels 1-5 since her laning damage is modest before crits come online — poke with Adaptive Strike (Agi) whenever she Stifling Dagger-harasses the wave. After level 6, track your HP carefully: a single Coup de Grâce crit from around 60% HP is a kill. Do not give PA free hits to probe your HP pool; shift into strength preemptively if she is level 6 and walking toward you.
